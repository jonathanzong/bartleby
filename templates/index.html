Testing Social Media Research Ethics Procedures

Principal Investigator: Elizabeth Paluck, Princeton University Department of Psychology, with J. Nathan Matias and Jonathan Zong.

What effects do people experience from having tweets removed for copyright reasons, and how can researchers study this issue ethically? Help us answer these questions by taking 15 minutes to answer our survey.

We are inviting you to take part in a research study. Before you decide to participate, it is important that you understand why the research is being done and what it will involve. Please read this information carefully. Ask us if anything is not clear or you need more information.

Purpose of the research

We are asking you to participate in our research because your tweets may have been removed for copyright reasons in the past, information that Twitter makes public on the Lumen Database. We plan to test new ways to support people like you, and we would like your feedback on the design, ethics, and privacy of our future study. This project is independent from Twitter and Lumen.

If you participate, what to expect

In this study, we ask you to imagine a situation where your tweets are removed by Twitter after a copyright complaint, and that we include your information in a study on the effects of online copyright enforcement.

Next, we will show you a website that allows people to manage the privacy of the information we collect for the study. We will ask for feedback on this website with a survey.

Benefits and risks

Your answers will help us improve how we manage the privacy of people who participate in our research. We will publish our findings when this study finishes. Follow our Twitter account to receive the results when we finish.

Confidentiality

All records from this study will be kept confidential. Only the research team will have access to your responses. Your responses will be kept private, and we will not include any personally-identifiable information in any publications.
